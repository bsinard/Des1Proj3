1. An idea is formed when you see something that compels you to create.

2. I create in bursts. I string together many ideas. Art does not always need to meet a purpose. To give meaning to an object without one is fruitless.

3. Assuming your work will be bad will make it bad. Self-hatred and humbleness are vastly different.

4. To analyze art you are still creating is to give something to nothing.

5. If it's stressing you out, you need to approach it differently. Art is a wild animal, it lashes out when nervous.

6. You do not owe your viewers an explanation for everything you create.

7. All perception of a creative work is inherently subjective. To open yourself to criticism is to give the floor to many differing voices. Diversity in opinion is very important.
